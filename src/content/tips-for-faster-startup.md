# Tips & Tricks to Speed Up Your PC Boot Time

Dealing with a slow boot-up time is frustrating and time-consuming. I used to start my PC, have coffee, breakfast, and come back *hoping* I had allotted enough time for it to turn on! There is no need to live like this. Your computer should be ready when you are, not the other way around. So here are my top tips to **speed up your PC’s boot time** and get you to what matters faster.

---

## Step 1: Disable Startup Programs

The first and easiest thing to do is **turn off programs that are hogging resources** upon startup.

### On Windows:
- Right-click on the **Windows Start** button → **Task Manager**  
- On the left-hand menu, click **Startup apps**  
- Look through the list. You’ll see a “Status” column and a “Startup Impact” column. The ones labeled as “Enabled” and marked with a “High” impact are your biggest boot-time offenders.  
- Right-click anything you *don’t need immediately when you log in* (think Spotify, OneDrive, Steam, etc.) and select **Disable**

Don’t worry, this doesn’t uninstall the program. It just stops it from running the moment your PC starts.

### On Mac:
- Go to **System Settings** → **General** → **Login Items**  
- You’ll see a list of apps set to run automatically at login  
- Click the **minus (-)** button to remove the ones you don’t want auto-launching

Be strategic. If you don’t use an app right after booting up, it doesn’t need to start with your system. **Less clutter = faster boot.**

---

## Step 2: Turn On Fast Startup (Windows Only)

This is kind of like putting your PC into hibernation instead of completely shutting it down. It lets your computer boot up quicker by **saving system information to a file**.

**Here’s how:**
1. Open **Control Panel** (Click Start & type "Control Panel" in the search bar) → **Hardware and Sound** → **Power Options**
2. Click **Choose what the power buttons do** (on the left)
3. Click **Change settings that are currently unavailable**
4. Under **Shutdown settings**, check the box that says **Turn on fast startup**
5. Save changes and reboot!

If you’re on an SSD, you might already boot quickly, but enabling this still helps **shave off seconds**.

---

## Step 3: Upgrade to an SSD (Solid State Drive)

If you're still using a traditional hard drive (HDD), you're essentially using tech from the dinosaur era. **Swapping it out for an SSD** is like trading in your tricycle for an E-bike. The difference is massive, not just in boot time, but in overall performance.

Most modern laptops already come with SSDs, but older desktops or budget machines might not. Good news: **SSDs are more affordable than ever**, and the upgrade process isn’t too hard if you’re comfortable with a screwdriver (or have a helpful tech friend nearby).

> **Boot times on HDDs:** 1–3 minutes  
> **Boot times on SSDs:** 10–20 seconds  

---

## Step 4: Keep Your System Updated

I know, updates can be annoying ~ especially when they pop up right as you're trying to shut down. But they actually help improve **boot performance** and fix security holes.

Make sure your:
- **Operating system** is up to date
- **Drivers** (especially for your graphics card and storage controller) are current
- On Windows, check for **optional updates** under:  
  `Settings → Windows Update → Advanced options → Optional updates`

---

## Step 5: Trim the Bloat

Some PCs come loaded with **bloatware** — random apps you didn’t ask for and never use (looking at you, Candy Crush). Uninstall them!

### On Windows:
- Go to **Settings** → **Apps** → **Installed apps**
- Review what you don't need. If you don’t recognize it or don’t use it, uninstall it.

### On Mac:
- Open **Finder**, go to **Applications**, and drag unused apps to the **Trash**

Clearing out unnecessary software not only helps your system boot faster but also **frees up space** and can make everything run smoother overall.

---

## Bonus Tip: BIOS/UEFI Boot Order

For the more adventurous: sometimes your computer is spending precious seconds checking for USB drives, CDs, or network connections before it boots your main drive.

To fix this:
- Enter your **BIOS/UEFI settings** during boot (usually by hitting `DEL`, `F2`, or `F12` when you power on)
- Look for **Boot Order** and make sure your SSD or main drive is listed **first**

---

## Final Thoughts

Getting your PC to boot faster doesn’t have to involve buying a brand new machine. With just a few tweaks, you can **cut your startup time significantly** and get back to doing whatever it is you *actually* turned your computer on for (gaming, work, doomscrolling ~ no judgment here).

If you try these tips and still feel like your PC is taking forever to wake up, it might be time for a deeper tune-up or even a hardware refresh. But 9 times out of 10, this list should be all you need.

Have fun speeding things up—and enjoy your coffee *after* your PC boots, not during.
